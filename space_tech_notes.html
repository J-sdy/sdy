🌐 What is an Orbit?
Definition: An imaginary path taken by a celestial body, spacecraft or satellite.
🔵 Classification 1: Shape (Elliptical / Circular)
  • Most objects follow an elliptical path; circle = special case
  • Perigee = Nearest point to Earth → speed Higher ↑
  • Apogee = Farthest point from Earth → speed Lower ↓
  • In circular orbit → distance & speed remain constant
🧠 Memory Trick
Perigee = Pull (gravity pulls harder, so faster) | Apogee = Afar (farther, slower)
🌍 Classification 2: Movement Around Earth
  • Geosynchronous Orbit: Syncs with Earth's rotation; distance can vary
  • Sun-Synchronous Orbit (SSO): Near-polar orbit; crosses same part of Earth at same local solar time → used for remote sensing & Earth observation
  • Geostationary Orbit: Special case of geosynchronous
  • Altitude: 35,784 km above equator
  • Satellite appears fixed at one point on Earth
  • Period: 24 hours
  • Uses: Communication & weather
  • Also called Clarke Orbit 🌟
📏 Classification 3: Distance from Earth
🔵 LEO
Up to 2,000 km
  • Earth observation
  • Remote sensing (SSO)
  • Metrology
  • Space stations
  • Hubble, Astrosat
🟢 MEO
2,000–36,000 km
  • Navigation satellites
  • GPS constellations
  • NavIC / IRNSS
🟡 GEO
~36,000 km
  • Communication sats
  • Fixed over one longitude
  • GSAT, INSAT series
🔴 HEO
Beyond 36,000 km
  • High Earth Orbit
  • Specialised missions

"Little Monkeys Go High" → LEO → MEO → GEO → HEO

⚙️ Propulsion Systems — Overview
🔴 Non-Air Breathing
Uses oxidizer to burn propellant
Example: Most chemical rockets
🟢 Air-Breathing
Uses atmospheric oxygen (no oxidizer carried)
Types: Ramjet & Scramjet
✈️ Ramjet Technology
  • Has a rotor blade + combustion chamber
  • Works at supersonic speeds (Mach 2–3)
  • ⚠️ Limited to speeds below hypersonic range
🚀 Scramjet Technology
Full form: Super Combustion Ramjet
  • Supersonic airflow compressed naturally by aircraft's forward speed
  • Operates at hypersonic speed (> Mach 5)
  • 1 Mach = 332 m/s
  • ✅ 2016: ISRO successfully tested Scramjet engine
  • 🔹 2024 Current Affairs: India–Japan collaborating on hypersonic vehicle research; DRDO + ISRO working on dual-mode ramjet-scramjet systems
FeatureRamjetScramjet
Airflow in combustorSubsonicSupersonic
Speed rangeMach 2–3> Mach 5 (hypersonic)
OxidizerAtmospheric O₂Atmospheric O₂
Rotor blade✅ Yes❌ No (ram compression)
❄️ Cryogenic Engine
  • Used in GSLV
  • "Cryo" = extremely low temperature (~–273°C)
  • Propellants: Liquid Hydrogen (20 K) + Liquid Oxygen (90 K)
  • ✅ Generates high thrust + clean tech (by-product: water)
  • ⚠️ Expensive but highly efficient
  • 🔹 2024–25: ISRO mastered cryo tech indigenously; CE-20 engine (used in LVM3) tested for 650 seconds
⛽ Propellants (Fuel) Comparison
TypeExample✅ Merits⚠️ Demerits
Solid HTPB (Hydroxyl-terminated polybutadiene) Easy to store & transport; stable Bulky; non-uniform / slow burning
Liquid UDMH (Unsymmetrical Dimethylhydrazine) Lightweight; uniform & fast burning Highly inflammable & corrosive; special handling needed
🧠 Remember
HTPB = Solid | UDMH = Liquid
"Humble Solid, Unstable Liquid" → solid is stable, liquid is volatile
🚀 ISRO Launch Vehicles — Overview
  • Launch Vehicles = Rockets that carry payloads into space
  • ISRO's LV programme began with:
    • → SLV (Satellite Launch Vehicle, 1980) — India's first indigenous rocket
    • → ASLV (Augmented SLV, 1987) — improved payload capacity
ELV – Expendable Launch Vehicle
Used once then discarded
✅ Simpler, reliable
❌ Expensive (non-reusable)
Examples: SLV, ASLV, PSLV, GSLV
RLV – Reusable Launch Vehicle
Designed to re-enter & be reused
✅ Up to 10× cheaper
✅ Sustainable & eco-friendly
Similar to NASA's Space Shuttle

☀️ Aditya-L1 — Solar Mission
India's 1st dedicated solar observatory | Launched 2023
  • Placed at Lagrange Point-1 (L1) — ~1.5 million km from Earth → stable vantage point
Objectives
  • ☀️ Solar Corona: Study heating & dynamics of outermost solar layer
  • 💨 Solar Winds & CMEs: Understanding origin & impact
  • 🧲 Magnetic Field Studies: How solar activity influences Earth's space weather
  • 📡 Space Weather Forecasting: Protect satellites, GPS, power grids, communication

  • 3 in-situ payloads → measuring solar wind, plasma, magnetic fields

🧭 NavIC / IRNSS — India's Navigation System
Full form: Navigation with Indian Constellation | Regional Navigation Satellite System by ISRO
  • Group of 7 satellites initially:
  • 4 in Geosynchronous Orbit
  • 3 in Geostationary Orbit
  • Coverage: Indian landmass + 1,500 km beyond borders
  • Technology: Each satellite carries 3 atomic clocks (Rubidium)
  • Accuracy: ~5–10 m (better than GPS ~20 m)
  • Applications: Civilian navigation + restricted defence applications
  • NVS-02 (2025): Latest satellite launched with rubidium atomic clocks
⭐ Significance of NavIC
  • 🛡️ Strategic Independence: Reduces reliance on GPS, ensures secure navigation
  • 🎯 Military Role: Missiles, drones, defense navigation
  • 🚗 Civilian Uses: Transport, aviation, disaster management, agriculture
  • 💰 Economic Role: Logistics, e-commerce, autonomous vehicles
  • 📱 Digital Economy: Enhances precise positioning services
  • 🌏 Global Recognition: Adopted in Sri Lanka, Nepal, Bhutan
  • 🔗 Expansion: Integration with GPS & Galileo for interoperability
  • 📱 Future: Global coverage planned; mandatory in smartphones from 2025

⚠️ GPS Spoofing
Definition: Cyber-attack using fake GPS signals to mislead receivers (aircraft, ships, vehicles) | Example: Reported use by Israel vs Iran (2024)
Implications
  • 🧭 Course Deviation: Aircraft/ships diverted from original path
  • ❌ Navigation Errors: False data in flight systems
  • ⚠️ Safety Risks: Misjudgment of altitude, speed → accidents
  • 🔒 Security Threats: Disruption of airports, military ops
  • 💸 Financial Losses: Flight delays, diversions, cancellations
  • 📉 Reputation Damage: Loss of trust in aviation safety
Way Forward
  • 🔐 Signal Authentication: Tech to verify & detect spoofed signals
  • 🔒 Encryption: Stronger GPS/NavIC encryption
  • 📡 Multi-Sensor Fusion: Radar + inertial navigation + ground aids
  • 📊 Real-time Monitoring: Track anomalies, issue alerts
🌍 International Space Station (ISS) — Facts
  • Launched: 20 November 1998
  • Permanently inhabited: Since 2000
  • Agencies: NASA (USA), Roscosmos (Russia), ESA (Europe), JAXA (Japan), CSA (Canada)
  • Role: Largest man-made structure in space
  • Size: Football-field scale; supports 6–7 astronauts
  • Orbit: 408 km altitude
  • Speed: 7.66 km/s | 1 orbit = 90–93 min (~16 orbits/day)
  • Cost: ~$15,000 crore USD (>$150 billion total)
  • Operational till: 2031 (planned deorbit in Pacific)
💡 Year Trick ISS launched 1998 → "ISS = 1 + 9 + 9 + 8 = old century ending, new era beginning"
✅ Significance of ISS
  • 🧪 Global science lab in microgravity → biology, physics, medicine, materials
  • 🌐 Symbol of international cooperation post-Cold War
  • 🤖 Technology demo for life-support, robotics, docking
⚠️ Challenges of ISS
  • Very costly (>$150 bn), aging structure, frequent resupply 💸
  • US–Russia tensions since 2022 🌐
  • Retirement planned by 2030 → deorbit in Pacific
Successors: NASA Lunar Gateway | China's Tiangong | India's BAS | Private stations (Axiom, Orbital Reef)

⚙️ Propulsion — Quick Recall

  • Non-air breathing: uses oxidizer | Air-breathing: uses atmospheric O₂
  • Ramjet: Mach 2–3 | Scramjet: >Mach 5 (hypersonic) | ISRO tested scramjet 2016
  • Cryogenic: LH₂ + LOX | Used in GSLV | Clean (water by-product)
  • Solid propellant: HTPB | Liquid propellant: UDMH

🚀 Launch Vehicles — Quick Recall

  • LVM3 (Bahubali): 3-stage; Chandrayaan-2 & 3, Gaganyaan

🧭 NavIC & GPS — Quick Recall

  • NavIC: 7 sats (3 GEO + 4 GSO); coverage India + 1500 km; accuracy 5–10m
  • Rubidium atomic clocks; mandatory in smartphones from 2025
  • GPS systems: USA(GPS), Russia(GLONASS), EU(Galileo), China(BeiDou), India(NavIC), Japan(QZSS)
  • GPS Spoofing: fake signals to mislead receivers; counter: multi-sensor fusion
