🏛️ Evolution & Basic Features

600–750 AD➡️ Nagara (North) + Dravida (South) defined➡️ After 750 AD➡️ Vesara (Deccan, hybrid)
  • ⚠️ 600–750 A.D. → Nagara (North India) & Dravida (South India) became clearly defined
  • ⚠️ After 750 A.D. → Vesara developed in Deccan region = hybrid of both
🧠 Mnemonic: "N-D-V" = North-Dravida-Vesara in time order: Nagara & Dravida first (600-750), Vesara later (hybrid, after 750).

🔑 Core Structural Elements (HIGH-YIELD for MCQs)

  • 🔹 Garbhagriha – innermost sanctum housing deity (small → later expanded) — asked in CHSL 17 Nov
  • 🔹 Mandapa – pillared hall/porch for devotees
  • 🔹 Shikhara/Vimana – tower above sanctum; curvilinear (N. India) / pyramidal (S. India)
  • 🔹 Vahana – deity's vehicle, placed axially in front + dhvaja (flagstaff)
  • 🔹 Pradakshina Patha – circumambulatory path around sanctum
  • 🔹 Dvarapalas – door guardians (Nagas, Yakshas, Nandi, Chanda-Prachanda, Ganga, Yamuna, sages)

⛰️ Nagara Style Temples

  • 🔹 Distinctive curvilinear Shikhara — symbolizes cosmic mountain Mount Meru
  • 🔹 Top crowned with Amalaka (ribbed disk) + Kalasha (finial) — ⭐ frequently asked pair
  • 🔹 ⚠️ Usually NO boundary walls / gopurams — focus on central tower
  • 🔹 Garbhagriha + mandapa(s) aligned on single axis
  • 🔹 Richly carved exteriors, simpler interiors

📜 Gupta Period Temples (4th–6th c. CE) — Formative Stage

  • Built in brick & stone (brick grew more prominent later)
  • Stone temples → relief sculptures; brick temples → carved bricks/terracotta panels
  • Eastern India: stucco later replaced by terracotta
Phase 1 — Modest square garbhagriha + flat-roofed portico
🔹 Eg: Parvati Temple, Nachna Kuthara
Phase 2 — Jagati (platform) + roofed pradakshina added
🔹 Eg: same Parvati Temple evolved
Phase 3 — Towering shikhara introduced; Panchayatana style
🔹 Eg: Deogarh Temple
Bhitargaon Temple (UP) — earliest true arch in India; brick temple; triratha plan — ⭐ unique fact
💡 Panchayatana = 1 main shrine + 4 subsidiary shrines on same platform (total 5 shrines → "Panch")

⏳ Timeline of Nagara Development

1. Gupta 4-6th c. Formation➡️ 2. Post-Gupta 6-8th c. Development➡️ 3. 8-10th c. Regional Expansion➡️ 4. 10-12th c. Maturity/Peak➡️ 5. 13th c.+ Decline

🏯 Khajuraho Temples (900–1130 CE) — Mature Nagara

  • Free-standing (no boundary walls)
  • Balconied windows with sloping chhajjas
  • Major: Kandariya Mahadeva, Lakshmana, Visvanatha Temples — ⭐ memorize all 3 names
  • Follows Panchayatana layout; each mandapa has own shikhara (clustered look)
  • Plan = Latin cross layout
  • 🔹 Famous for extensive, intricate erotic sculptures — ⭐ classic exam giveaway clue for Khajuraho

Source: GS Astra Siddhi Prep — Temple Architecture, pgs 3–6

🌊 Odisha Temples (7th c. AD)

  • Distinct regional style; tower called Deul — rises vertically then curves sharply inward at top
  • Mandapa (assembly hall) called Jagamohana — ⭐ very commonly tested term
  • Features: square compartments/niches, lavish carvings, boundary walls present

☀️ Konark Sun Temple

  • Built 13th c. CE (~1250 CE) by Narasimhadeva I (Eastern Ganga Dynasty)
  • Dedicated to Surya (Sun God); shaped as giant stone chariot
  • 🔹 24 wheels (12 pairs) + 7 horses; wheels work as sundials — ⭐ most-asked Konark fact
  • Main sanctum (deul) collapsed; Jagamohana still stands
  • Built using Khondalite stone
  • Known as "Black Pagoda" by European sailors
  • Oriented East (first sun rays); UNESCO World Heritage Site (1984)
🧠 24 wheels = 24 hours/fortnights; 7 horses = 7 days of week ☀️

🏯 Ayodhya Ram Mandir (Quick Facts)

  • Traditional Nagara style, 3-storey, pink sandstone (Mirzapur & Bansi-Paharpur, Rajasthan)
  • Dimensions: 250 ft wide × 161 ft tall; covers 2.67 acres; 71 acres total complex
  • 390 pillars, 46 doorways, 5 mandaps
  • Garbh Griha houses Ram Lalla idol; also Rang Mandap & Nritya Mandap
  • 💡 Sun rays focus on idol every Ram Navami noon via mirror-lens system — no electricity needed
  • Idol (51 inches) sculpted by Mysuru sculptor Arun Yogiraj
  • ⚠️ No iron used in construction — built to last 1000+ years

🗿 Dravida Style Temples

Flourished in South India, 8th – 13th/14th c. AD

  • 🔹 Vimana: stepped, 6 or 8 sides; topped by round Stupi (finial)
  • 🔹 Shikhara: pyramidal, progressively smaller storeys
  • 🔹 Gopurams: massive ornamental gateways (added later); with pillared halls/corridors
  • 🔹 Garbhagriha: square sanctum within large covered enclosure
  • 🔹 Entire temple enclosed within compound wall

🏯 Pallava Temples (4th–9th c. CE)

Pioneers of Dravidian architecture — shift from rock-cut caves → structural stone temples. Established vimana, mandapa, sculpted gateways.

4 Phases (Pallava Architectural Styles) — ⭐ high-yield sequence

1. Mahendra Style (610–630 AD)
Rock-cut mandapas in mountain faces; massive square pillars, octagonal mid-shaft
🔹 Eg: Mandagapattu, Mahendravadi, Trichy caves
2. Mamalla Style (630–668 AD)
Free-standing Monolithic Rathas + open-air bas-reliefs
🔹 Eg: Pancha Rathas Mahabalipuram, Arjuna's Penance
3. Rajasimha Style (690–800 AD)
Shift to structural temples (stone+mortar); towering vimanas
🔹 Eg: Kailasanathar Temple Kanchipuram, Shore Temple Mahabalipuram
4. Nandivarman Style (800–900 AD)
Decline phase, smaller refined temples; bridge to Chola era
🔹 Eg: Vaikunta Perumal Temple, Muktesvara Temple Kanchipuram
🧠 Mnemonic: "My Mom Really Naps" → Mahendra → Mamalla → Rajasimha → Nandivarman
💡 Pancha Rathas = 5 monolithic rathas named: Dharmaraja, Bhima, Arjuna, Sahadeva, Draupadi

🔱 Chola Temples (c. 850–1200 CE)

Perfected large-scale structural stone temples; massive granite construction, towering vimanas, refined sculpture.

Phase 1: Early (small)➡️ Phase 2: Zenith (Rajaraja I & Rajendra I)➡️ Phase 3: Later (ratha-shaped mandapas)

Phase 1 — Early

  • Square vimana + Ardha-mandapa in front; 8 sub-shrines = Astaparivaralya
  • 🔹 Eg: Vijayalaya Choleeswaram

Phase 2 — Zenith (Rajaraja I & Rajendra I)

  • Exquisite sculptural ornamentation
  • 🔹 Brihadisvara Temple, Thanjavur — built of large granite stones; tallest vimana 66m, 16 storeys — ⭐ most-asked Chola fact

Phase 3 — Later

  • Additional pillared mandapas, some shaped like ratha (wheeled chariot) drawn by elephants/horses
  • 🔹 Eg: Airavatesvara Temple, Darasuram

🏯 Brihadeeswara Temple — Key Facts

  • Built 1010 CE by Rajaraja Chola I; dedicated to Shiva
  • Also called Rajarajesiwaram / Peruvudaiyar Temple; known as "Dakshina Meru"
  • 🔹 First fully granite temple in the world; ~130,000 tons granite; completed in just 5 years
  • Main tower (Vimana/Srivimana): ~66m, 14 storeys; dome from single ~25ft granite block
  • Sanctum: 28m square, houses 8.7m Shiva Linga
  • Gopurams: Keralantakan Gate (main, 30m high), Rajarajan Gate, Tiruvamukam Gate
  • Massive monolithic Nandi (2nd largest in India); fresco paintings with secular themes
  • UNESCO World Heritage (Great Living Chola Temples, 1987)

🏯 Airavatesvara Temple, Darasuram

  • Built by Rajaraja Chola II, 12th c. CE (c.1146–1173 CE)
  • Dedicated to Shiva (Airavatesvara); named after Airavata (Indra's white elephant)
  • 🔹 Famous chariot-shaped (ratha) mandapa with carved stone wheels
  • 🔹 Musical steps — produce different musical notes when tapped — ⭐ unique distinguishing fact
  • Part of UNESCO Great Living Chola Temples

🔀 Vesara Style Temples

  • Developed under Chalukyas of Kalyani
  • Fusion of Nagara (North) + Dravida (South)
  • Vimana: early phase flat/sloping roofs → later tower-like
  • Pillared mandapa added in front of four-walled garbhagriha
  • Towers: modified Dravidian towers, reduced height per level, diminishing storeys
  • 🔹 Distinct: more vertical profile (unlike inclined Nagara storeys)
  • Examples: Durga Temple Aihole, Meguti Temple Aihole, Virupaksha Temple Pattadakal

⭐ Hoysala Temples (12th–13th c.)

Mysore plateau; highly distinctive style — also classed as Vesara/Hybrid (Dravida+Nagara)

  • 🔹 Stellate (star-shaped) plans — complex platforms, multiple projections
  • Material: soft soapstone (chloritic schist) — allows extremely fine carvings
  • Layout: Garbhagriha → Antrala (vestibule) → Navaranga/Gudha-mandapa
  • 🔹 Eg: Hoysaleshvara Temple (c.1150 CE), dark schist stone

3 UNESCO Sites (inscribed 2023, 42nd India site)

Chennakeshava (Belur)
Built by Vishnuvardhana, commemorates victory over Cholas, 1116 CE
Hoysaleswara (Halebidu)
Largest of 3; double temple to Shiva; detailed outer wall sculptures
Keshava (Somanathapura)
Trikuta (3-shrine) temple; high-density detailed carvings
💡 Themes depicted: Hindu epics, mythology, animals, dance & music; lathe-turned pillars are unique.

📍 Kailashnath Temple, Ellora

  • Dynasty: Rashtrakuta (King Krishna I, 756–773 AD)
  • Location: Aurangabad, Maharashtra (part of 34 Ellora Caves)
  • Technique: Monolithic, carved top-down from single rock
  • 🔹 Largest monolithic structure in the world; dedicated to Shiva — ⭐ most-asked fact
  • Style: Dravidian; massive pillars, life-sized elephants in courtyard
  • Depicts scenes from Ramayana & Mahabharata
  • ⚠️ Don't confuse with Kailasanathar Temple, Kanchipuram (built by Pallava king Narasimhavarman II/Rajasimha)

📍 Kamakhya Temple, Assam

  • Location: Nilachal Hill, Guwahati; dates to 8th–9th c. (Mleccha/Pala dynasty); original destroyed 1565, rebuilt by Koch Dynasty
  • Style: Nilachala Style = Nagara (curvilinear shikhara) + Saracenic/Mughal (domed roofs, arches)
  • Primary site of Tantric practices & Shakti tradition
  • 🔹 No idol — deity worshipped as yoni-like cleft in bedrock with natural spring — ⭐ unique fact
  • Mythology: linked to falling of womb/genitals of Sati
  • Ambubachi Mela — annual 4-day festival marking goddess's menstruation

📍 Modhera Sun Temple

  • Finest example of Nagara style in Gujarat; built 1026 CE by Bhimdev I (Solanki Dynasty)
  • Dedicated to Surya; large rectangular stepped tank = Surya Kund
  • Ornate Torana (arched gateway) leads to Sabha Mandapa (open on all sides)
  • Intricate carvings reflect Gujarati woodcarving tradition

📍 Angkor Wat (Cambodia)

  • 🔹 Largest religious monument in the world; covers 200 acres — ⭐ most-asked fact
  • Built by Khmer King Suryavarman II, ~1110–1150 (~900 years old)
  • Originally dedicated to Vishnu → became Buddhist temple by end of 12th c.
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site (1992)

🎨 Buddhist Schools of Art

Buddhism grew rapidly in 1st–2nd c. CE → 3 main sculpture schools: Gandhara, Mathura, Amaravati

🕉️ Aniconic Phase (initial)

  • Buddha shown indirectly through symbols, not physical form (avoided idol worship)
Symbols of Buddha's Life Events — ⭐ HIGH-YIELD TABLE
EventSymbol
Janma (Birth)Lotus & Bull
Mahabhinishkramana (Renunciation)Horse
Nirvana/Sambodhi (Enlightenment)Bodhi tree
Dharmachakra pravartana (1st Sermon)Wheel
Mahaparinirvana (Death)Stupa

🟤 Gandhara School

  • NW India (~Pakistan, near Peshawar); under Indo-Greek kings, later Sakas & Kanishka
  • Strong Greek/Roman influence → also called Graeco-Buddhist School
  • Influences: Greek, Roman, Persian, Saka, Kushan
  • Features: wavy hair, long nose, slim oval face; bold togas-like drape; jewelry & mustache sometimes; Greek pantheon figures appear too
  • 🔹 Found at: Begram (Afghanistan), Taxila, Jalalabad, Hadda — ⭐ marked as PYQ in source
  • 🔹 Bamiyan Buddhas = example of this style

🔴 Mathura School

  • Independent indigenous tradition; mainly red sandstone; flourished under Kanishka
  • Depicts Vaishnava, Shaiva, Jain Tirthankara & Buddhist images
  • Key centers: Mathura (main), Sarnath, Kausambi
  • Features: round smiling faces, relaxed flesh; fully clothed, transparent robe quality; left shoulder covered; decorated halo

⚪ Amaravati School

  • South India, Krishna river banks; under Satavahana Dynasty
  • Key feature: "Narrative art" — circular medallions show full stories continuously
  • Centers: Amaravati, Nagarjunakonda, Goli, Ghantasala, Vengi
  • Sensuous, flowing, rounded forms; dance/music/daily-life scenes from Jatakas
  • Includes religious + secular images; Stupa built of fine white limestone
  • Later influenced Pallava & Chola architecture

🛕 Stupas

Definition: Dome-shaped sacred monument containing Buddha's/sacred relics.

  • Term in Rigveda ("estuka"), Atharvaveda; Pali "thupa" = conical heap/shrine with relic
  • Originally a funerary mound (5th c. BCE) → evolved into religious monument
  • Earliest = simple hemispherical earth/brick mounds → later elaborate, carved

👑 Mauryan Era — Ashoka

  • 8 stupas built before Ashoka to enshrine Buddha's cremated remains at key life-sites
  • Ashoka (268–232 BCE) redistributed relics → built thousands of stupas to spread Buddhism
  • Later stupas also honored arhats, bodhisattvas, local deities
  • Shift: non-iconic → iconic practice with rise of Mahayana

🏗️ Stupa Structure (Parts) — ⭐ MOST-ASKED DIAGRAM

Medhi→ Anda→ Harmika→ Yasti→ Chhatras
  • 🔹 Medhi – square base platform, surrounded by Vedika (boundary wall)
  • 🔹 Anda – hemispherical dome enclosing relic casket
  • 🔹 Harmika – square railing atop dome (sacred enclosure)
  • 🔹 Yasti – central spire/mast = axis mundi (cosmic axis)
  • 🔹 Chhatras – parasols above yasti, symbolize protection & teachings
  • 🔹 Toranas – ornate gateways at cardinal directions, tied to Buddha's life events
  • 🔹 Pradakshina Patha – circumambulatory path around medhi
🧠 Mnemonic: "My Aunt Has Yellow Chairs" → Medhi-Anda-Harmika-Yasti-Chhatras

🌏 Regional Styles

  • Sanchi → elaborate carvings & reliefs
  • Amaravati → intricate sculptures
  • Ladakh/Tibet → distinctive Tibetan style: white dome + colourful banners

📰 In News: Piprahwa Stupa

  • Sacred Piprahwa relics of Buddha returned to India (public-private effort: Govt of India + Godrej Industries Group)
  • Discovered 1890s at Piprahwa Stupa by William Peppe
  • Officially excavated 1898, in UP — believed to be ancient Kapilavastu (Buddha's birthplace)
  • Relics: bone fragments, crystal caskets, gold ornaments, ritual offerings
  • Brahmi script inscription links relics to Buddha, deposited by the Sakya clan

⛏️ Buddhist Caves — Overview

Rock-cut architecture, 3rd c. BCE – 6th c. CE → monastic residences (Viharas) + prayer halls (Chaityas)

📅 Key Phases of Evolution — ⭐ exam favourite

Initial Phase (3rd c. BCE)
By Ashoka, for Ajivika sect
🔹 Barabar Caves (Bihar) — earliest, polished walls, simple layout
Hinayana Phase (2nd c.BCE–2nd c.CE)
Western India (Maharashtra); symbolic Buddha worship (Stupa)
🔹 Chaityas: Bhaja(earliest), Bedsa, Karle
🔹 Viharas: Nasik/Pandavleni, early Ajanta
Mahayana Phase (5th–6th c. CE)
Idol worship introduced; Buddha statues in chaityas/viharas
🔹 Eg: Later Ajanta caves 16,17,19,26
Final Phase (6th–10th c. CE)
Multi-storeyed, complex; blend of Buddhist+Hindu+Jain
🔹 Eg: Ellora Caves

✅ Quick Revision Summary

🛕 Temple Architecture

  • Nagara = North, curvilinear shikhara, no gopuram/boundary wall
  • Dravida = South, pyramidal vimana, gopurams + compound walls
  • Vesara = Deccan hybrid, Chalukyas of Kalyani
  • Odisha: Rekha/Pidha/Khakra Deul; Konark = Sun Temple chariot, 24 wheels, 7 horses, 1250 CE
  • Pallava (4 phases) → Chola (granite, Brihadisvara 66m tallest vimana, 1010 CE) → Hoysala (star-shaped, soapstone, 12-13th c.)
  • Garbhagriha = sanctum; Mandapa = hall; Shikhara/Vimana = tower; Gopuram = gateway (South only)
  • Khajuraho = 900–1130 CE, Chandela, erotic sculptures, Latin cross plan

☸️ Buddhist Architecture

  • 3 Schools: Gandhara (Greek-influenced, grey stone) | Mathura (red sandstone, indigenous) | Amaravati (white marble, narrative medallions)
  • Stupa parts: Medhi → Anda → Harmika → Yasti → Chhatras → Torana
