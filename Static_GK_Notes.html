🌍 Overview of Indian Classical Music
  • One of the oldest & most complex musical traditions — unbroken lineage of 3,000+ years
  • Essentially a melodic & rhythmic art form — focuses on a single melody, unlike Western music (harmony/chords)
  • Three main pillars: Raga, Tala, Swara

📅 Five Phases of Indian Music

Phase 1 — Vedic & Ancient Foundations 2000 BCE – 400 CE
Focus: Origins of sound and classification
  • Samaveda — Root of Indian music; transformed Rigvedic verses into musical chants
  • Margi Sangita — "Sacred Way"; ancient music rooted in Vedic rituals → leads to Moksha
  • Natyashastra (Bharata Muni) — Most critical ancient treatise
    • Grouped instruments into 4 categories: Tata (string), Sushira (wind), Avanaddha (membrane), Ghana (solid)
    • Jatis — Melodic prototypes before modern "Ragas"
  • Silappadikaram (by Ilango Adigal) — Ancient Tamil epic; earliest evidence of Kerala & Tamil Nadu musical heritage (e.g., Maddalam)
🧠 Mnemonic for 4 Instrument Categories (TSAG):
Tata (String) · Sushira (Wind) · Avanaddha (Membrane) · Ghana (Solid)
→ "Teach Students About Ghana"
Phase 2 — Medieval Evolution & Bifurcation 500 CE – 1500 CE
Focus: Rise of Ragas & North/South split
  • Brihaddesi (Matanga Muni) — First text to explicitly define the term Raga
  • Sangeet Ratnakara (Sarangadeva) — 13th-century foundational text; written in Deccan (Maharashtra); last common authority for both Hindustani & Carnatic
  • Amir Khusro (13th C) — Introduced Persian & Arabic elements; credited with Qawwali, Khyal; refined Sitar & Tabla
  • Haridasa Movement (Karnataka) — Promoted Devara Nama (devotional songs in Kannada) → groundwork for Carnatic tradition
Phase 3 — Golden Era of Bhakti & Courts 1500 CE – 1800 CE
Focus: Patronage, Gharanas & Carnatic Trinity
  • Tansen (16th C) — Legendary musician of Akbar's court; associated with Raga Miya Malhar & Dhrupad singing
  • Venkatamakhi (17th C) — Authored Chaturdandi Prakashika; formulated 72-Melakarta system (backbone of Carnatic music)
  • Carnatic Trinity (Late 18th C) — Tyagaraja, Muthuswami Dikshitar, Syama Sastri; lived in Tiruvarur; elevated the Kriti form
  • Annamacharya (15th C) — Composed over 32,000 Sankirtanas in Telugu
🧠 Mnemonic — Carnatic Trinity: "TMS"
Tyagaraja · Muthuswami Dikshitar · Syama Sastri
All born in Tiruvarur → "TMS lived in Tiru"
Phase 4 — Modern Systematization 1800 CE – 1950 CE
Focus: Schools of music & standardized notation
  • Gharana System: Gwalior (oldest), Agra (Dhrupad-influenced), Kirana, Jaipur-Atrauli (complex ragas)
  • Pt. Vishnu Narayan Bhatkhande — Classified Hindustani Ragas into 10 Thaats; introduced modern Notation System (e.g., Kramik Pustak Malika)
  • Pt. Vishnu Digambar Paluskar — Democratization of music via public concerts; founded Gandharva Mahavidyalaya
  • Dwaram Venkataswamy Naidu — Popularized violin in South India; pioneer of Carnatic violin
Phase 5 — Contemporary & Global Era 1950 CE – Present
Focus: Innovation & Global recognition
  • Pandit Ravi Shankar — Pupil of Ustad Allauddin Khan (Maihar Gharana); took Sitar global; collaborated with Western artists
  • Pandit Shivkumar Sharma — Transformed Santoor from Kashmiri folk to mainstream classical
  • Ustad Bismillah Khan — Elevated Shehnai to classical status
  • UNESCO Recognition — Baul tradition (West Bengal) & Vedic Chanting → Intangible Cultural Heritage

⚡ Quick Revision — Music History

  • Samaveda → root of music | Natyashastra → 4 instrument categories (TSAG)
  • Brihaddesi → 1st defines "Raga" | Sangeet Ratnakara → last common authority
  • Amir Khusro → Qawwali, Khyal, Sitar, Tabla | Tansen → Akbar's court, Raga Miya Malhar
  • Venkatamakhi → 72-Melakarta | Carnatic Trinity → TMS in Tiruvarur
  • Bhatkhande → 10 Thaats | UNESCO → Baul & Vedic Chanting

🎵 Anatomy of Swaras

🎼 Saptak & Notes
  • An octave in Indian music = Saptak
  • Total notes: 12 notes (7 pure + 5 altered)
  • Shruti — Smallest interval (microtone); 22 Shrutis in one Saptak
  • Swara — "Self-shining" pitch; 7 main shrutis become Shuddha Swaras

7 Shuddha Swaras (Pure Notes)

#NoteFull NameAnimal 🐾
1SaShadja🦚 Peacock
2ReRishabh🐂 Bull/Skylark
3GaGandhar🐐 Goat
4MaMadhyam🦢 Heron/Crane
5PaPancham🐦 Cuckoo
6DhaDhaivat🐎 Horse/Frog
7NiNishad🐘 Elephant
🧠 Mnemonic (Animals): "Pretty Rascals Go Mad Pulling Dog's Nose"
Peacock · Bull · Goat · Heron · Cuckoo · Horse · Elephant

Classification by Movement

  • Achal Swara (Fixed) — Sa & Pa — No variants; anchors of the scale
  • Vikrit Swara (Altered) — Remaining 5 notes change pitch:
    • Komal (Flat) — Re, Ga, Dha, Ni (semi-tone lower)
    • Teevra (Sharp) — Only Ma becomes Teevra Madhyam (semi-tone higher)
💡 Trick: Sa & Pa never change → Stable Pillars. Only Ma goes Sharp (Teevra). Re, Ga, Dha, Ni → "Really Good Dancers Naturally" go Komal (flat).

🎶 Anatomy of Ragas

🎶 What is a Raga?
  • Raga = comprehensive melodic framework: scale + specific notes + emotional mood

1. Jati — Classification by Note Count

JatiNotes Used
Audav5 notes
Shadav6 notes
SampurnaAll 7 notes
  • A Raga can have different Jatis for Aaroha & Avaroha → e.g., Audav-Sampurna = 5 notes up, 7 down

2. Vadi & Samvadi — The Hierarchy

TermRoleDescription
Vadi👑 KingMost important note; played most frequently; defines character
Samvadi🤝 Minister2nd most important; usually 4 or 5 notes away from Vadi
Anuvadi👥 SubjectsAll other notes (not Vadi or Samvadi)
Vivadi⚔️ EnemyStrictly forbidden / used very sparingly

3. Time Theory — Samay Chakra

  • Indian Classical Raagas are assigned to specific times of day or seasons (unlike Western music)
  • Sandhiprakash Raagas — Performed at twilight (sunrise or sunset)
  • Seasonal Raagas: Raag Megha Malhar (Rainy season), Raag Basant (Spring)

4. Structural Components

  • Aaroha — Ascending order of notes (Sa, Re, Ga...)
  • Avaroha — Descending order (Ni, Dha, Pa...)
  • Pakad — Unique catch-phrase; immediately identifies the Raga
  • Thaat — "Parent scale" from which Raga is born (10 Thaats in Hindustani music)

5. Thaat vs Raga — Key Differences

BasisThaatRaga
OriginScale from 12 swarasFrom a specific Thaat
NotesExactly 7 notesAt least 5 notes
TypesOnly AarohaBoth Aaroha & Avaroha
MelodyNot melodious; never sungMelodious; always sung
SwarasNo Vaadi/SamvaadiMust have Vaadi & Samvaadi
NamingAfter popular RagasAfter emotion evoked

Bilawal · Khamaj · Kafi · Asavari · Bhairavi · Bhairav · Kalyan · Marwa · Poorvi · Todi

ComponentDescription

⏱️ Anatomy of Tala

⏱️ What is Tala?
  • Tala = rhythmic cycle that measures time; provides temporal structure

Components of Hindustani Tala

ComponentMeaningKey Point
MatraBeatSmallest time unit; Teental=16, Dadra=6
VibhagMeasure/SectionTala divided into sections (e.g., Teental = 4 Vibhags of 4 each)
SamFocal Point1st beat; most important; denoted by X
KhaliEmpty BeatNo heavy accent; hand wave; denoted by 0
TaaliClapAccented beats; marks beginning of Vibhag; numbered 2, 3...
LayaTempoVilambit (slow), Madhya (medium), Drut (fast)
AvartanCycleOne complete execution of Tala (Sam to last beat)
🔸 Teental Structure: 16 matras → 4 Vibhags × 4 beats = Sam(X) on 1st, Taali on 5th & 13th, Khali(0) on 9th

⚡ Quick Revision — Anatomy

  • 12 total notes | 7 Shuddha | Sa & Pa = Achal (fixed) | Only Ma = Teevra
  • Raga: Jati (5/6/7 notes) | Vadi=King | Samvadi=Minister | Vivadi=Enemy

  • Practiced in Northern India
  • Emphasizes musical structure & improvisation
  • Gharana system — links musicians by lineage or style

🪗 Carnatic Music — Overview
  • Classical tradition of South India
  • Vocal-centric — compositions written to be sung
  • Kriti-based — Kriti = highly evolved song set to specific raga & fixed tala
  • Great importance on Sahitya (quality & meaning of lyrics)
  • Languages: Telugu, Kannada, Tamil, Sanskrit

🎶 Components of Carnatic Composition

PartDescription
PallaviFirst/second thematic lines; repeated in each stanza; main platform for improvisation
Anu PallaviTwo lines after Pallavi; sung at beginning & end; not strictly repeated every stanza
CharanaFinal and longest verse; concludes the piece
🔸 Carnatic Improvisation:
  • Ragam — Melodic improvisation without mridangam
  • Tanam — Melodic improvisation with mridangam in free rhythm

🎼 Melakarta System

  • Melakarta Ragas — 72 "parent" ragas (formulated by Venkatamakhi)
  • Janya Ragas — "Child" ragas derived from Melakarta ragas

💃 What Makes a Dance "Classical"?
  • Must adhere to Natya Sastra principles
  • Considered a form of Yajna (sacrifice) / moving meditation
  • Three components: Nritta (Pure Dance), Nritya (Expressive Dance), Natya (Dramatic Element)
ComponentMeaningFocus
NrittaPure DanceRhythmic movements; no emotion; posture, footwork, speed
NrityaExpressive DanceAbhinaya (expressions) + Mudras (hand gestures)
NatyaDramatic ElementTheatrical aspect; characters & narrative
🧠 4 types of Abhinaya (Expression): "A Asks Sameer Vachana"
Angika (body) · Aaharya (costume) · Satvika (emotional) · Vachika (speech/song)

📜 Historical Timeline of Classical Dance

Ancient Period (2nd C BCE – 10th C CE)
  • Dance = "Fifth Veda"; spiritual medium for all
  • Nātya Śāstra (Sage Bharata) — first "grammar" of dance; Rasas, Mudras
  • Devadasi tradition — Women educated in Sanskrit, music, dance; performed Kumbha Arati
  • Gupta "Golden Age" — Dance as essential education for royalty (Kalidasa's Malavikagnimitram)
Medieval Period (11th – 18th C)
  • Bhakti Movement — biggest influence; devotion to Krishna/Rama made dance narrative
  • Kathak — temple storytelling → courtly art under Delhi Sultanate & Mughals
  • Sattriya — Sankaradeva (Assam) developed in monasteries to spread Vaishnavism
  • Chola & Vijayanagara — 108 Karanas carved in temple walls (e.g., Brihadeshwara Temple, Thanjavur)
  • Abhinaya Darpana & Gita Govinda — shifted focus to Sringara Rasa
Period of Decline (British Era)
  • Colonial administrators viewed dances as "erotic/backward"
  • Anti-Nautch Movement — Victorian era; sought to ban temple dances; traditions survived in rural households
Classical Renaissance (Mid-20th C)
  • Pioneers: E. Krishna Iyer, Rukmini Devi Arundale, Vallathol Narayana Menon
  • Kalakshetra (Bharatanatyam) — Rukmini Devi Arundale
  • Kerala Kalamandalam (Kathakali) — standardized curriculum
  • Sangeet Natak Akademi — officially recognized 8 classical dances

💃 The 8 Classical Dances — Detailed

🌟 Bharatanatyam Tamil Nadu
  • Based on Natyashastra & Abhinaya Darpana
  • Precursor: Sadirattam; banned by British in 1910
  • Known as Agninritya (Fire Dance)
  • Traditionally performed by families in Tanjore; descendants = Nattuvans
  • Ekharya — Solo dancer performing multiple roles
  • Sequence: Pushpanjali → Alarippu → Jatiswaram → Shabdam → Varnam → Padam → Tillana
  • Schools: Melattur, Pandanallur, Vazhuvoor, Kalakshetra
  • Key Artists: Alarmel Valli, Yamini Krishnamurthy, Padma Subrahmanyam, T. Balasaraswati, Leela Samson, Sonal Mansingh
🌟 Kathak Uttar Pradesh
  • Founded by Ishwari Prasad
  • Weight equally divided on horizontal & vertical axes
  • Strong influence of Persian costumes
  • Jugalbandi — competitive play between dancer & tabla
  • Revival: Leela Sokhey

🌟 Kuchipudi Andhra Pradesh
  • Originated from Kuchelapuram village; revived by Siddhendra Yogi
  • Themes: Bhagavata Purana; Shringar Rasa; Carnatic music
  • Unique Elements:
    • Manduka Shabdam — Frog story
    • Tarangam — Dancing on edges of brass plate
    • Jal Chitra Nritya — Drawing pictures with toes while dancing
  • Key Artists: Yamini Krishnamurthy, Radha Reddy, Raja Reddy, Indrani Rehman, Guru Vempati Chinna Satyam
🌟 Odissi Odisha
  • Originated from temple dances of Devadasis; supported by King Kharavela
  • Historical Sites: Udayagiri & Khandagiri caves
  • Musical Accompaniment: Pakhawaj
  • Kelucharan Mohapatra — First Odissi dancer to receive Padma Vibhushan (2000)
  • Key Artists: Sonal Mansingh, Sanjukta Panigrahi, Indrani Rehman
🌟 Kathakali Kerala
  • Mixed form of dance, music & acting; prevalent in Malabar, Cochin, Travancore
  • Also known as 'ballad of the east'
  • Literary Connection: Nalacharitham play
  • Uses facial colors to symbolize characters
  • Music: Sopana Sangeetham (Kerala's indigenous music)
  • Instruments: Chenda, Madhalam, Chengila, Ilathalam (sometimes Idakka)
  • 8 Parts of Kathakali Performance: Keli → Arangu Keli → Thodayam → Vandana Slokam → Purappadu → Melappadam → Kadhabhinayam → Dhanarasi
  • Key Artists: Guru Gopinath, Mrinalini Sarabhai, Vallathol Narayana Menon
🌟 Mohiniyattam Kerala
  • Theme: Dedicated to Vishnu (Mohini avatar)
  • Language: Manipravalam (Sanskrit + Malayalam mix)
  • Textual Reference: Ancient text Vyavahara Mala
  • "Mother of Mohiniyattam": Kalyanikutty Amma
  • Main Percussion: Edakka
  • Key Artists: Hema Malini, Sridevi, Kanak Rele, Sunanda Nair
🌟 Manipuri Manipur
  • Themes: Lord Krishna; introverted & graceful
  • Main Components: Jagoi & Cholom
  • Forms: Raas, Sankirtana, Thang-Ta
  • Instruments: Pung & Dhol
  • Key Artists: Jhaveri Sisters (Naina, Suwarna, Ranjana, Darshana), Guru Bipin Singh
🌟 Sattriya Assam
  • Introduced by saint Srimanta Sankardev; recognized as classical dance in 2000
  • Themes: Mythological stories of Lord Vishnu
  • Musical Composition: Borgeet
  • Technical Element: Mati-Akhora (basic exercises)
  • Key forms: Chali (graceful), Jhumura (vigorous/masculine), Nadu Bhangi
  • Also includes: Gayan-Bhayanar Nach & Kharmanar Nach

⭐ Must-Remember Festival Facts
  • Hornbill Festival (Nagaland) → "Festival of Festivals" → Dec 1–10 → Kisama Heritage Village, Kohima
  • Wangala (Meghalaya/Garo tribe) → "100 Drums Festival"
  • Attukal Pongala (Kerala) → World's largest gathering of women devotees
  • Tulip Festival (Srinagar) → Asia's largest tulip garden near Dal Lake
  • Ganesh Chaturthi → Popularized by Bal Gangadhar Tilak during freedom movement
  • Hola Mohalla (Punjab) → Started by Guru Gobind Singh
  • Baisakhi → Both harvest festival & foundation day of Khalsa Panth
  • Onam (Kerala) → State festival; King Mahabali; Pookalam + Vallamkali + Onasadya
  • Bihu (Assam) — 3 types: Rongali(April/New Year), Kongali(October), Bhogali(January/Harvest)
  • Pongal (Tamil Nadu) — 4 parts: Bhogi → Thai Pongal → Mattu Pongal → Kanum Pongal
  • Kambala (Karnataka) — Buffalo race; Tulu-speaking coastal communities
  • Sohrai (Jharkhand) — Famous for Sohrai wall paintings (Santhal tribe)

⚡ Quick Revision — Festivals

  • Hornbill = "Festival of Festivals" (Dec 1-10, Nagaland, Kisama Village)
  • Wangala = 100 Drums Festival (Garo tribe, Meghalaya)
  • Attukal Pongala = world's largest women gathering (Kerala)
  • Tulip Festival = Asia's largest tulip garden (Dal Lake, Srinagar)
  • Bihu → 3 types (Apr/Oct/Jan) | Pongal → 4 parts
  • Hola Mohalla → Guru Gobind Singh | Ganesh Chaturthi → Bal Gangadhar Tilak
  • Kambala = buffalo race (Karnataka) | Sohrai = wall paintings (Jharkhand)
  • Kharchi = 14 deities worship (Tripura, July)
