📌 Ch1: Basic Economic Problem

Scarcity→Choice→Trade-off→Opportunity Cost→3 Central Questions

1️⃣ Scarcity

  • Limited resources + Unlimited wants = Scarcity
  • ✅ Scarcity ≠ Shortage — it is a permanent mismatch
  • 🔹 ₹500 but want shoes, bag & headphones → can't buy all

2️⃣ Opportunity Cost ✅ (Most Asked)

Value of the NEXT BEST alternative forgone when a choice is made
⚠️ OC is NOT total cost | NOT money spent | NOT all alternatives — ONLY NEXT BEST
  • 🔹 Choose SSC over ₹20,000/month job → OC = ₹20,000

📌 Ch4: Production Possibility Frontier (PPF)

PPF = Max combinations of 2 goods economy can produce with available resources & technology
LocationMeaning
On curve✅ Efficient
Inside curve⚠️ Inefficient (unemployment/waste)
Outside curve❌ Unattainable
  • PPF is concave (bowed outward) — resources not equally efficient
  • PPF shifts outward: Tech↑, Resources↑, Education↑, Capital investment↑
  • PPF shifts inward: War, disaster, loss of resources

📌 Law of Demand

Price ↑ → Qty Demanded ↓ | Price ↓ → Qty Demanded ↑ (ceteris paribus)
TypeCauseExample
Movement along curveChange in pricePrice of milk ↓ → buy more milk
Shift of curveChange in non-price factorsHealth trend → demand ↑ at same price

Factors Shifting Demand

  • Income: ↑ income → ↑ demand (normal) | ↑ income → ↓ demand (inferior)
  • Taste & Preference: Favorable change → demand ↑
  • Population: ↑ population → ↑ demand

  • Longer time → more elastic | Habit-forming goods (cigarettes) → inelastic

📌 Consumer Theory

Utility & Marginal Utility

  • Utility: Satisfaction from consuming a good
  • MU: Additional satisfaction from ONE MORE unit
Law of Diminishing MU: As consumption ↑ → MU from each unit ↓
ConditionResult
MU > 0TU increases
MU = 0TU is MAXIMUM ✅
MU < 0TU decreases

Indifference Curve

  • Shows combos of 2 goods giving same satisfaction
  • Properties: Downward sloping | Convex to origin | Higher curve=higher satisfaction | Never intersect
  • Also called: Iso-utility curve
Consumer Equilibrium: MU_A/P_A = MU_B/P_B (equal MU per rupee)

📌 Business Cycle & Keynesian Economics

Recovery→Expansion→Boom (Peak)→Recession→Depression→Recovery
PhaseKey Features
BoomHigh growth, strong demand, low unemployment, near full capacity
RecessionGDP falls 2 consecutive quarters, rising unemployment, declining investment
DepressionSevere & prolonged recession; sky-high unemployment. Ex: Great Depression 1929-33
RecoveryDemand↑, investment↑, employment improves

Keynesian Tools

  • Fiscal Stimulus: Govt spending↑ → Income↑ → Consumption↑ → Jobs↑
  • Public Works: Roads, railways, irrigation — immediate job creation
  • Liquidity Preference: 3 motives — Transaction | Precautionary | Speculative
  • Liquidity Trap: Interest rates so low → people prefer holding cash over investing
Economic SituationKeynesian Policy
Recession/Depression↑ Govt spending, ↓ taxes (Expansionary)
Boom/High Inflation↓ Spending, ↑ taxes (Contractionary)
📌 Phillips Curve: Inverse relationship between inflation and unemployment

⚡ Module 1–2 (Micro): Quick Revision

  • ✅ Scarcity = limited resources + unlimited wants | OC = NEXT BEST alternative forgone
  • ✅ Robbins=Scarcity | Smith=Wealth | Marshall=Welfare | Keynes=Employment
  • ✅ PPF: On=efficient | Inside=inefficient | Outside=unattainable | Concave shape
  • ✅ Barter problem #1 = Double Coincidence of Wants
  • ✅ Law of Demand: inverse relationship (ceteris paribus) | Price→movement | Non-price→shift
  • ✅ Elastic >1 | Inelastic <1 | Unit=1 | TR max at unit elastic
  • ✅ Giffen=inferior | Veblen=status | Snob=exclusivity | Bandwagon=herd | Demonstration=imitation
  • ✅ MU=0 → TU is maximum | Iso-utility curve = Indifference curve
  • ✅ Qd=Qs=Equilibrium | Qd>Qs=Shortage(price↑) | Qs>Qd=Surplus(price↓)
